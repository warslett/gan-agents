# 11 Awesome Scout Activities You Have to Try This Week

**Source:** https://www.bigmaninthewoods.co.uk/blog/11-awesome-scout-activities-you-have-to-try-this-week/

## Extracted Information

A UK-based scouting blog with 11 practical activities that can be run at weekly meetings. Mix of crafts, cooking, games, and leadership development.

### 1. Easy S'mores Craft
- Materials: modelling clay, cork tiles
- Create non-edible s'mores keepsakes
- Teaches fine motor skills with a creative twist on a camping classic
- Good for younger scouts

### 2. Mini Drum Set from Scraps
- Materials: scraps of wood, basic tools
- Build percussion instruments from reclaimed wood
- Demonstrates resourcefulness: turning waste into something fun and functional
- Introduces fundamental woodworking techniques

### 3. Whittled Gnomes and Hedgehogs
- Materials: wood, carving knives, nails for details
- Carve small woodland creatures with nail embellishments
- Combines knife safety with creative craft
- Scouts take home as keepsakes

### 4. Patrol Leaders Academy
- Focus: leadership development (not a craft)
- Develops leadership skills within the troop
- Investment in young leaders to strengthen group dynamics

### 5. Mini Golf Course from Recycled Materials
- Materials: various recyclable items
- Construct a playable golf course from discarded materials
- Teaches creativity, promotes environmental awareness, and provides an entertaining game

### 6. Fun Spinning Peanuts
- Materials: craft supplies, peanuts, flexible wire
- Fashion spinning toys from simple components
- Simple yet entertaining; often becomes a memorable meeting highlight

### 7. Easiest Campfire Pizza
- Materials: Dutch oven, pizza ingredients
- Prepare pizza outdoors using a Dutch oven
- Merges outdoor cooking skills with universally loved food
- "Practically guaranteed to be a hit at any scout camp"

### 8. Don't Let the Ball Drop Challenge
- Materials: balls, team coordination
- Cooperative game requiring coordination and communication
- Groups work together to prevent balls from dropping
- Promotes teamwork and problem-solving

### 9. Summer Flowers from Wool on a Fork
- Materials: wool, forks
- Craft decorative flowers using everyday items as tools
- Demonstrates how everyday items can become creative tools

### 10. Blow Art Campfire Designs
- Materials: paint, straws, paper
- Create campfire-themed artwork using breath-controlled paint techniques
- Every piece is unique and original

### 11. Cork Bugs and Beyond
- Materials: corks, craft supplies
- Construct creatures from cork with open-ended creativity
- Simple materials but encourages unlimited creativity
- Challenge: "What other creatures can your scouts create?"
