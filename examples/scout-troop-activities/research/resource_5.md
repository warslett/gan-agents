# Scout Activity Ideas to Try This Year

**Source:** https://ventureabroad.co.uk/blog/scout-activity-ideas-to-try-this-year/

## Extracted Information

A UK-focused guide covering both indoor and outdoor scout activities, with connections to badge requirements and programme planning.

### Indoor Activities

#### 1. Scout Skills Stations
- Rotating stations covering map reading, first aid basics, and knot tying
- Aligns with UK Scouts Programme requirements
- Can contribute toward challenge badges
- Good for structured meeting evenings

#### 2. DIY Scout Craft Activities
- Bug hotels from recycled materials
- Paracord woggles
- Friendship bracelets
- Paper campfires
- Develops fine motor skills
- Connects to environmental conservation badgework

#### 3. Indoor Sleepover or Camp-In
- Set up mock camping in scout halls using sleeping bags and pop-up tents
- Activities include: indoor s'mores, campfire sing-alongs, shadow puppet theatre, themed treasure hunts
- Builds confidence before real camps
- Good stepping stone for nervous campers

#### 4. Badge-Focused Workshop Evenings
- Dedicated sessions covering specific badge content
- Examples: "Personal Safety," "Digital Citizen," "Money Skills"
- Can include guest speakers from local emergency services or financial sectors
- Combines learning with badge progression

### Outdoor Activities

#### 5. Local Nature Scavenger Hunt
- Explore nature reserves or green spaces
- Find items: oak leaves, pinecones, animal prints, birds
- Connects to Naturalist and Environmental Conservation badges

#### 6. Community Clean-Up Walk
- Combine hiking with litter collection
- Use gloves and high-visibility vests
- Counts toward Community Impact badge work
- Teaches civic responsibility

#### 7. Outdoor Cooking Challenge
- Scouts plan and prepare meals using camping gear
- Examples: foil packet meals, campfire toasties, bannock bread
- Supports Cook Activity Badge requirements

#### 8. Orienteering or Map Quest
- Navigation courses using local parks with checkpoints
- Suited to different age groups with varying difficulty
- Develops map and compass skills
- Aligned with Navigation badges

#### 9. DIY Obstacle Course or Wide Game
- Patrol-based activities using natural materials
- Themed games like "Rescue the Explorer"
- Emphasises teamwork
- Adaptable difficulty levels for mixed-age groups
