# Summary — Hiking and History Travel Destination

## 1. Original Problem

> "Where should I go travelling this year if I enjoy hiking and history?"

**Scoring Dimensions:** Fun, Cost, Challenge, Safety, Climate

## 2. Final Idea

### Peru — The Inca Trail and Sacred Valley

A three-week trip centred on the four-day Inca Trail to Machu Picchu, with extended hiking and historical exploration across the Sacred Valley, Cusco, and beyond. The final proposal includes a detailed 18-21 day itinerary designed around graduated altitude acclimatisation (Lima at sea level, Sacred Valley at 2,800m, Cusco at 3,400m, then the Inca Trail reaching 4,215m). It covers the Inca Trail permits and logistics ($600-800 USD, book 4-6 months ahead), a thorough treatment of altitude sickness risks and mitigation, safety advice for Cusco and Peruvian transport, an optional Lares Trek supplement, and a complete budget estimate ($2,070-3,240 USD for 3 weeks).

**Why it survived:** Peru consistently scored highest on Fun (9/10) and Challenge (9/10) across both criticism rounds. It offers the most transformative travel experience of the three candidates — the combination of arriving at Machu Picchu on foot, high-altitude trekking, deep cultural immersion in Quechua communities, and world-class cuisine creates a trip of genuinely higher impact than the alternatives. Its weaknesses (safety concerns around altitude sickness and petty crime, moderate total cost) were acknowledged honestly and addressed with concrete mitigation strategies through the revision process.

## 3. Ideas Overview

Three ideas were generated in Round 1:

1. **The Lycian Way, Turkey** -- 540km Mediterranean coastal trail threading through 2,500 years of Lycian, Greek, and Roman ruins. Outstanding value for money.

2. **The Scottish Highlands** -- West Highland Way combined with Highland historical sites (Glencoe, Culloden, Stirling). Safe, accessible, English-speaking.

3. **Peru — Inca Trail and Sacred Valley** -- Four-day Inca Trail to Machu Picchu plus Sacred Valley exploration. High-altitude challenge with deep cultural immersion.

## 4. Evolution Through Rounds

### Peru — The Inca Trail and Sacred Valley (Idea 3) -- WINNER
- **Round 1:** Aggregate 7.6. Exciting and ambitious proposal. Criticised for significantly understating altitude sickness risk, not mentioning the Inca Trail permit system and advance booking requirement, casually presenting the Ausangate Circuit as an easy add-on, and insufficiently qualifying cost claims.
- **Round 2 (Revision):** Thoroughly revised with comprehensive altitude sickness section, acclimatisation strategy, Diamox recommendation, permit logistics, and transparent $600-800 USD trek cost. Reframed Ausangate as a serious separate undertaking. Aggregate rose to 7.8. Safety improved from 5 to 6. Criticised for lacking a structured day-by-day itinerary.
- **Round 3 (Final Revision):** Added complete 18-21 day itinerary structured around graduated acclimatisation, itemised budget estimate ($2,070-3,240 USD total), and cold-weather packing advice. The itinerary itself demonstrates the acclimatisation strategy rather than merely describing it.

### The Lycian Way, Turkey (Idea 1)
- **Round 1:** Aggregate 7.4. Strong initial proposal emphasising the trail's integration of hiking and ancient ruins. Criticised for glossing over climate variability (late May heat spikes), failing to mention inconsistent waymarking, and not addressing safety in remote sections with no mountain rescue.
- **Round 2 (Revision):** Added specific timing guidance (early-to-mid April), detailed navigation advice (GPS with GPX tracks, Kate Clow guidebook), safety recommendations (PLB, satellite communicator, travel insurance), and a language section. Aggregate rose to 7.8. Climate improved from 7 to 8; Safety from 6 to 7. Remaining criticism: water availability on mountain sections, surface-level history coverage. Evicted in Round 2.

### The Scottish Highlands (Idea 2)
- **Round 1:** Aggregate 6.0. Presented as safe and accessible with rich history. Heavily criticised for omitting midges (biting insects endemic June-September), understating rain frequency (15-20 rain days per month in western Highlands), high costs, very low challenge for an English-speaking culturally familiar destination, and poor integration of hiking with historical sites. Evicted in Round 1.
