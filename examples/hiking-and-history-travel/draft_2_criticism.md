# Criticism of Draft 2

## Idea 1: The Lycian Way, Turkey

### Scores

| Dimension | Score (1-10) |
|---|---|
| Fun — How much day-to-day enjoyment and excitement will the trip provide? | 8 |
| Cost — How much experience and quality do you get relative to what you spend? | 9 |
| Challenge — How much will the trip push you out of your comfort zone overall (language, culture, navigation)? | 7 |
| Safety — How low-risk is the destination across all factors: crime, health, terrain, and infrastructure? | 7 |
| Climate — How suitable is the climate for hiking? Temperatures hotter than 25 degrees Celsius will be uncomfortable. | 8 |
| **Aggregate** | **7.8** |

### Critical Analysis
This revision is materially improved. The three major criticisms from Round 1 — climate risk, navigation issues, and safety/remoteness — have all been addressed directly and honestly. The timing advice to target early-to-mid April is specific and correct. The navigation section now explicitly recommends GPS with GPX tracks and the Kate Clow guidebook, which transforms a real risk into a manageable one. The safety section's recommendation of a PLB or satellite communicator is practical and appropriate for the remoteness involved. The language section is new and balanced.

The climate score improves from 7 to 8 because the revised timing guidance (April rather than the vaguer "spring") significantly reduces the chance of uncomfortably hot days. The safety score improves from 6 to 7 because the proposal now honestly acknowledges the remoteness risk and provides concrete mitigation (PLB, insurance, awareness of hospital locations).

Remaining weaknesses are minor. The proposal could mention water availability — some sections of the trail have long stretches between water sources, particularly on the mountain segments, and carrying 2-3 litres is necessary. The description of the history, while compelling, remains somewhat surface-level: it lists sites but does not suggest how to learn about them (e.g., hiring a local guide for key ruins, reading a history of Lycia before the trip). The fun score holds at 8 rather than rising because the day-to-day experience on the Lycian Way, while rewarding, involves long solitary walking through scrubland and pine forest between the highlight ruins — not every day is spectacular. This is honest trail walking, not a curated tourist experience, and the proposal could better set that expectation.

### Verdict: Strong

## Idea 2: Peru — The Inca Trail and Sacred Valley

### Scores

| Dimension | Score (1-10) |
|---|---|
| Fun — How much day-to-day enjoyment and excitement will the trip provide? | 9 |
| Cost — How much experience and quality do you get relative to what you spend? | 7 |
| Challenge — How much will the trip push you out of your comfort zone overall (language, culture, navigation)? | 9 |
| Safety — How low-risk is the destination across all factors: crime, health, terrain, and infrastructure? | 6 |
| Climate — How suitable is the climate for hiking? Temperatures hotter than 25 degrees Celsius will be uncomfortable. | 8 |
| **Aggregate** | **7.8** |

### Critical Analysis
This revision is substantially improved. The three key criticisms from Round 1 — altitude sickness understatement, missing permit logistics, and the Ausangate Circuit being casually presented — have all been addressed thoroughly. The altitude sickness section is now excellent: it provides the acclimatisation strategy (fly to Lima, acclimatise in Sacred Valley before Cusco), mentions Diamox, and appropriately warns against dismissing the risk. The permit and cost information is now transparent, with the $600-800 USD figure clearly stated. The Ausangate Circuit is correctly reframed as a serious separate undertaking.

The safety score improves from 5 to 6 because the proposal now includes specific practical advice (money belt, registered taxis, reputable bus companies). However, it cannot rise higher because the underlying risks — altitude sickness, petty crime, road safety — are inherent to the destination and cannot be mitigated away entirely. A 25-50% rate of some degree of altitude sickness is a substantial health risk that no amount of good planning can eliminate, and this honestly limits the safety score.

The cost score holds at 7. While Peru is cheap on the ground, the total trip cost is significant when you factor in: international flights (typically $800-1,200 from Europe), the Inca Trail guided trek ($600-800), and the need for 2-3 weeks of travel to justify the long journey and allow proper acclimatisation. The "affordable" framing is now more honest, but the total outlay for a complete Peru trip remains moderate-to-high.

The fun score remains at 9 — deservedly so. Every day of this trip offers something different: city exploration in Cusco, market visits, ruin explorations, mountain passes, cloud forest, and the culminating arrival at Machu Picchu. The cultural immersion is genuine and the sense of achievement is profound.

One new concern: the proposal is now quite long and detailed, which is good for planning but may overwhelm. A suggested itinerary with day-by-day structure would help translate this information into actionable plans.

### Verdict: Strong

## Eviction Recommendation

**Evict: Idea 1 — The Lycian Way, Turkey**

**Reason:** Both ideas are now strong and closely matched at 7.8 aggregate. The deciding factors are Fun (Peru 9 vs Turkey 8) and Challenge (Peru 9 vs Turkey 7). Peru offers a more transformative, once-in-a-lifetime experience — the combination of Machu Picchu, high-altitude trekking, and deep cultural immersion across language, cuisine, and living indigenous traditions creates a trip of genuinely higher impact. Turkey's advantages in Cost (9 vs 7) and Safety (7 vs 6) are real but represent smaller margins than Peru's advantages in the experiential dimensions. The Lycian Way is an excellent trip, but Peru is the more extraordinary one. For a traveller who values being pushed, moved, and changed by their journey, Peru is the stronger recommendation.
