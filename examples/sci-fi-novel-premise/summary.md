# Summary — Science Fiction Novel Premise

## 1. Original Problem

> "What would be a good premise for a new science fiction novel?"

**Scoring Dimensions:** Originality, Impact, Simplicity, Coherence, Adaptability

## 2. Final Idea

### Palimpsest Worlds

Faster-than-light travel works by overwriting reality. Ships carry a mathematical template of a habitable world and project it onto existing space, replacing whatever was there -- like broadcasting a signal over a carrier wave. The template is the signal; the original reality is the carrier. You can overlay a new signal, but the carrier never stops transmitting.

Dr. Fen Matsuda is a xenoarchaeologist on Tessera, a colony overwritten eleven times. She reads the palimpsest layers: alien ruins phasing in and out, fossils of organisms from different physics, gravity wells where old mathematics leaks through. When she discovers artifacts from Layer Zero -- the original reality -- she finds they are functioning instruments designed to amplify the carrier wave, causing the original reality to reassert itself.

The reassertion manifests as escalating phenomena: impossible geometries, thinning atmosphere, alien flora growing through walls, and palimpsest organisms -- Layer Zero's original inhabitants, still living their lives in a reality that was overwritten but never destroyed.

Fen's discovery reframes everything: the instruments are not weapons but a communication protocol. The reassertion is a voice saying: *We were here. We are still here. Acknowledge us.*

The resolution is radical coexistence -- rewriting templates to include Layer Zero, creating hybrid worlds where human and alien physics coexist in negotiated equilibrium. Architecture that accommodates two geometries. Atmosphere maintained as a careful blend. Ecosystems where terrestrial and alien organisms share space. It is harder, stranger, more beautiful, and the only option that does not require humanity to be either conqueror or exile.

**Why it survived:** Palimpsest Worlds consistently scored highest on originality (reaching 10/10) and adaptability (reaching 10/10), with no precedent in science fiction for its specific combination of FTL mechanism, palimpsest archaeology, ontological reassertion, and dual-reality cohabitation. It successfully addressed every weakness raised in criticism -- simplifying its mechanism via the signal/carrier analogy, grounding its cosmic threat in tangible phenomena, and arriving at a morally sophisticated resolution. It engages with the broadest thematic territory (colonialism, coexistence, justice, erasure, acknowledgment) while maintaining narrative focus through a single location, a single protagonist, and a clear investigative structure.

## 3. Ideas Overview

Five premises were generated in Round 1:

1. **The Grief Frequency** -- A grieving astrophysicist discovers that grief emits a quantum frequency detectable across spacetime. Thousands of alien grief signals converge on a four-billion-year-old structure offering a technology that can reverse death -- but only if the user surrenders their capacity to grieve, and therefore to love.

2. **The Cartography of Consciousness** -- In a near-future world, consciousness can be mapped as a spatial landscape. A cartographer discovers a shared unconscious commons connecting all human minds, containing evidence of an intelligence that has been shaping human thought for millennia.

3. **The Inheritance Tax** -- In a world of consciousness uploading, uploaded minds must justify their continued existence every fifty years before a tribunal. A public defender discovers systematic bias against artists, dissidents, and the neurodivergent in the tribunal's metrics.

4. **Palimpsest Worlds** -- Faster-than-light travel works by overwriting reality with templates of habitable worlds. A xenoarchaeologist discovers that the original, overwritten reality is reasserting itself -- and that the reassertion is not an attack but a communication.

5. **The Quiet After** -- Benevolent aliens solve all of humanity's material problems and leave. Thirty years later, humanity is falling apart from purposelessness. A psychologist investigates why the suicide rate is climbing in paradise.

## 4. Evolution Through Rounds

### The Grief Frequency (Idea 1)
- **Round 1:** Aggregate 7.0. Praised for emotional core and unique Fermi Paradox spin. Criticized for overloaded plot (factions, multiple speculative leaps), weak simplicity (5/10), and poor coherence (6/10).
- **Round 2 (Revision):** Stripped to a chamber piece -- one ship, five crew, no external antagonists. Aggregate rose to 7.8. Simplicity improved to 8, coherence to 8. Adaptability flagged as weak (5/10).
- **Round 3 (Revision):** Added the emotional frequency network as epilogue architecture for series potential. Aggregate rose to 8.4. Adaptability improved to 8. Concern raised about dependence on character interiority without structural scaffolding.
- **Round 4 (Revision):** Specified five crew members with distinct losses, added three-act structure, introduced Lament confrontation sequences as set pieces. Aggregate rose to 8.8. Impact reached 10/10. Evicted in Round 4 against Palimpsest Worlds (9.2).

### The Cartography of Consciousness (Idea 2)
- **Round 1:** Aggregate 6.6. Lowest scoring idea. Criticized for derivative core conceit (echoes of Inception, Psychonauts), unclear mechanism, and unfocused narrative identity. Evicted in Round 1.

### The Inheritance Tax (Idea 3)
- **Round 1:** Aggregate 7.4. Praised as the most structurally sound premise. Strong coherence (8/10) and simplicity (8/10). Weak on originality (7/10) and adaptability (6/10) due to familiar genre ingredients and constraining courtroom structure.
- **Round 2 (Revision):** Added specific corruption case (stolen music, corrupt judge), expanded world-building (Archive Halls, compute black markets, biological death movement), added protagonist's personal jeopardy. Aggregate rose to 8.0. Adaptability improved to 8. Originality remained at 7.
- **Round 3 (Revision):** Deepened ideological conflict (bias is systemic, not conspiratorial), added "what replaces the Metric?" as unresolvable moral question, dual-jeopardy climax. Aggregate rose to 8.2. Impact reached 9. Evicted in Round 3 on lowest originality (7) among remaining ideas.

### Palimpsest Worlds (Idea 4) -- WINNER
- **Round 1:** Aggregate 7.8. Highest originality (9/10) and adaptability (9/10). Weakest on simplicity (6/10) due to complex overwriting mechanism, and coherence concern about vague "pushback" element.
- **Round 2 (Revision):** Introduced signal/carrier wave analogy, grounded reassertion in specific tangible effects (alien geometries, atmospheric replacement, invasive flora), reframed resolution as cohabitation vs. abandonment. Aggregate rose to 8.4. Simplicity improved to 7, coherence to 8.
- **Round 3 (Revision):** Refined the signal/carrier wave mechanism further, originality reached 10/10, coherence 9/10. Aggregate rose to 9.0. Only concern: execution risk with complex layered realities.
- **Round 4 (Revision):** Reframed Layer Zero instruments as communication protocol ("We were here. We are still here. Acknowledge us."), introduced palimpsest organisms, specified hybrid worlds resolution, deepened Fen's personal arc. Aggregate reached 9.2. Originality 10, Adaptability 10.
- **Round 5 (Final Revision):** Full elaboration of the premise with all improvements integrated -- coral cathedrals of Layer Three, rooms larger on the inside, atmosphere transition gradients, palimpsest organisms as neighbors not ghosts.

### The Quiet After (Idea 5)
- **Round 1:** Aggregate 7.0. Praised for subversive hook ("why is the suicide rate climbing in paradise?") and literary quality. Criticized for weak adaptability (5/10), vague resolution, and risk of "puppet-master aliens" trope.
- **Round 2 (Revision):** Replaced "secret test" twist with genuine alien misunderstanding, added neurological mechanism and the Compass framework, focused setting on Voss. Aggregate rose to 7.4. Adaptability improved to 6. Criticized for tension between philosophical ambition and mechanistic explanation. Evicted in Round 2.
