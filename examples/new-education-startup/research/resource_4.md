# Education Technology Market Analysis - Grand View Research

**Source:** https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/education-technology-market

## Extracted Information

### Market Size and Growth

- **2024 Market Size:** USD 163.49 billion
- **2025 Projected Size:** USD 187.02 billion
- **2030 Target:** USD 348.41 billion
- **Growth Rate:** CAGR of 13.3% (2025-2030)

### Sector Breakdown

- **K-12 segment** dominated with 38-39% market share in 2024, driven by game-based learning adoption
- **Preschool** represents the fastest-growing sector, leveraging mobile assessment tools and touchscreen-enabled learning devices
- The K-12 dominance suggests this is where most buying happens, but preschool growth suggests an emerging opportunity

### End-Use Segments

- **Business applications** led with approximately 66% revenue share, reflecting strong corporate training adoption
- **Consumer segment** shows fastest growth potential due to demand for adaptive, flexible learning solutions
- Corporate/business training is the revenue heavyweight, not traditional academic education

### Component Types

- **Hardware** captured over 39% of revenue through interactive displays, laptops, and simulation equipment
- **Content delivery** is expanding fastest as e-learning platforms offer study materials, test prep, and educational videos
- Software and content are where growth is heading, even though hardware still captures significant revenue

### Deployment Models

- **On-premises solutions** maintained largest market share due to data security and compliance requirements in education
- **Cloud deployment** grows fastest, offering reduced costs, mobile accessibility, and rapid implementation
- Schools and institutions are cautious about cloud due to student data privacy concerns

### Regional Performance

- **North America** leads with 35.62% share, supported by venture capital investment
- **Asia Pacific** shows fastest expansion, driven by affordable broadband in developing nations like India, enabling broader educational access
- Asia Pacific growth suggests massive underserved populations coming online for the first time

### Key Market Drivers

- AI and personalized learning integration
- Virtual/augmented reality implementations
- Government digital literacy initiatives
- Mobile learning platform adoption
- Gamification in K-12 education
- Increasing demand for upskilling and reskilling in the workforce
