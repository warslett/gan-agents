### Getting There - Flight Hub Airports

- **Scotland:** Edinburgh (EDI), Glasgow (GLA) - well connected to European budget airlines
- **Norway:** Bergen (BGO), Oslo (OSL), Stavanger (SVG)
- **Austria:** Innsbruck (INN - limited), Salzburg (SZG), Vienna (VIE - major hub) + Munich (MUC) for Tyrol
- **Switzerland:** Zurich (ZRH), Geneva (GVA), Basel (BSL)
- **Slovenia:** Ljubljana (LJU - small), Venice (VCE) 2 hours away, or Klagenfurt (KLU)
- **Romania:** Bucharest (OTP), Cluj-Napoca (CLJ), Sibiu (SBZ)
- **Czech Republic:** Prague (PRG - major hub)
- **Portugal:** Porto (OPO)
- **Basque Country:** Bilbao (BIO)
- **Iceland:** Keflavik (KEF)

### Car vs. Public Transport for Hiking Access

**Best with car rental:**
- Scotland (Highlands poorly served by bus)
- Ireland (rural areas limited public transport)
- Romania (mountain trailheads remote)
- Norway (fjord areas, though ferries help)

**Excellent public transport to trailheads:**
- Switzerland (PostBus network reaches almost every valley; Swiss Travel Pass covers trains, buses, boats)
- Austria (efficient train and bus network to mountain villages; cable cars included in regional passes)
- Czech Republic (train network to national parks)
- Slovenia (manageable by bus from Ljubljana, but car adds flexibility)
- Basque Country (good regional trains and buses)

### Mountain Hut Systems

**Switzerland:** SAC (Swiss Alpine Club) huts. Must book in advance in peak season. 40-70 CHF for half-board (dinner + breakfast). Clean, well-maintained. Dorm-style sleeping.

**Austria:** OeAV/DAV huts. Similar system to Swiss. 30-50 EUR half-board for members. Alpine Club membership (50-70 EUR/year) gives discounts across Alps.

**Norway:** DNT (Norwegian Trekking Association) huts. Self-service and staffed options. Key system for self-service huts (deposit for key). 200-500 NOK per night.

**Scotland:** Bothies (free, basic shelters) plus B&Bs along popular routes. No hut system like Alps.

### Safety and Difficulty Considerations

**Beginner-friendly destinations (well-marked, lower altitude, good infrastructure):**
- Hadrian's Wall Path (flat to rolling)
- Ireland (Kerry Way, Wicklow Way)
- Czech Bohemian Switzerland
- Camino de Santiago
- Rota Vicentina, Portugal

**Intermediate (some altitude, longer days, good paths):**
- Scottish Highlands
- Norwegian fjord hikes
- Austrian Tyrol day hikes
- Slovenian Julian Alps
- Romanian Carpathians

**Advanced (high altitude, exposed terrain, navigation skills needed):**
- Tour du Mont Blanc
- Swiss high routes
- GR20 Corsica
- Norwegian Trolltunga
- Carpathian ridge routes

### Shoulder Season Advantages

**Spring (April-May):**
- Wildflowers at lower elevations
- Fewer crowds
- Lower prices (20-30% less than peak)
- Snow still on high passes (limits some alpine routes)
- Longer daylight than winter

**Autumn (September-October):**
- Autumn colours spectacular (especially Dolomites, Bavaria, Scotland)
- Stable weather in many regions
- Fewer crowds than summer
- Lower prices
- High alpine routes still accessible in September
- Shorter days than summer but still adequate
