# European Climate Guide: Avoiding Hot Weather While Travelling

**Source:** Compiled from widely available travel and climate knowledge (multiple sources inaccessible due to site restrictions)

## Extracted Information

### Understanding European Climate Zones

**Mediterranean (Southern Europe):** Greece, southern Italy, southern Spain, southern France, Croatia coast, Turkey. Hot dry summers (30-40C July/August). NOT recommended for heat-averse travellers in summer. However, spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) are excellent with 18-25C.

**Oceanic (Western/Northwestern Europe):** UK, Ireland, Brittany, Netherlands, Belgium, northern Spain coast, northern Portugal coast. Mild, damp year-round. Summers 15-22C. Rarely uncomfortably hot. Best for heat-averse travellers.

**Continental (Central Europe):** Germany, Poland, Czech Republic, Austria (valleys), Hungary. Warm summers (22-30C) but not extreme. Mountain areas much cooler. Shoulder seasons good.

**Alpine:** Swiss Alps, Austrian Alps, Dolomites, Pyrenees, Scandinavian mountains. Cool at altitude regardless of season. Summer 10-20C depending on elevation. Excellent for heat-averse hikers.

**Subarctic/Nordic:** Scandinavia, Iceland. Cool summers (10-20C). Long daylight. Generally excellent for avoiding heat.

### Coolest European Destinations in Summer (ranked)

1. **Iceland** - 10-15C average highs, virtually never hot
2. **Northern Norway/Sweden (above Arctic Circle)** - 12-18C
3. **Faroe Islands** - 11-14C, windy, dramatic landscapes
4. **Scottish Highlands** - 14-19C
5. **Ireland west coast** - 15-19C
6. **Norwegian fjord region** - 15-20C
7. **Swiss/Austrian high Alps (above 2000m)** - 10-18C
8. **Northern England (Lake District, Yorkshire)** - 17-20C
9. **Southern Sweden/Denmark** - 19-23C
10. **Brittany, France** - 19-23C

### Best Months for Each Strategy

**If travelling in summer (June-August):**
- Choose northern destinations (Scandinavia, Scotland, Ireland, Iceland)
- Choose high-altitude destinations (Swiss Alps, Austrian Tyrol, Dolomites)
- Avoid Mediterranean basin entirely

**If flexible on timing:**
- **April-May:** All of Europe is pleasant. Southern Europe ideal (18-25C). Wildflowers in bloom.
- **September-October:** Mediterranean cools down. Central Europe beautiful with autumn colours. Still warm enough for hiking most places.
- **November-March:** Limited hiking options due to snow/short days, except mild coastal areas (Canary Islands, Algarve, Azores)

### Heat Risk by Region and Month

**Destinations that stay under 25C even in peak summer:**
- Iceland, Faroe Islands
- Scottish Highlands
- Norway (coast and north)
- Ireland
- Swiss Alps above 1,800m
- Austrian Alps above 1,800m

**Destinations 25-30C in peak summer (warm but tolerable, especially mornings/evenings):**
- Northern France, Belgium, Netherlands
- Germany
- Poland
- Czech Republic
- Baltic States
- Northern Spain coast (Basque Country, Galicia)
- Northern Portugal
- Slovenia (valleys)
- Romania (mountain areas)
